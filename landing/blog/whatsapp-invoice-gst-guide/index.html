WhatsApp Invoice Sharing: A GST-Compliant Guide for Indian SMBs

The most common question we hear from accountants: "Is sending a GST invoice on WhatsApp even legal?" Short answer: yes, and it has been since the GST framework took effect. The longer answer matters when an audit shows up. Here is what the rules actually require, and how to set up a compliant WhatsApp invoice workflow that doesn't bite you later.

What the GST law actually says about delivery

Section 31 of the CGST Act and the corresponding Rule 46 spell out what an invoice must contain — supplier GSTIN, recipient details, HSN code, taxable value, tax rate, signature or digital signature, and so on. Neither the Act nor the Rules dictate the channel of delivery. As long as the recipient receives a copy of the prescribed invoice, hand-delivery, courier, email and WhatsApp are all valid.

Rule 48(4) further allows electronic invoices in lieu of physical ones for B2B supplies above the e-invoicing threshold, with the IRN and QR code generated from the IRP portal. A WhatsApp PDF that carries the IRN-stamped invoice is therefore as compliant as a printed copy handed over the counter.

What a compliant WhatsApp send actually looks like

  • The PDF itself is the compliance object. The format must contain every field Rule 46 lists. TallyPrime's default sales invoice template covers this out of the box; if you run a customised template, double-check the GSTIN, HSN, place of supply and signature blocks.
  • Retain the source. The original voucher must remain in the seller's books for the statutory retention period (six years under Section 36). Sending it on WhatsApp does not replace storage — it supplements it. TallyPrime does the heavy lifting here automatically.
  • IRN where applicable. If you cross the e-invoicing threshold, generate the IRN in TallyPrime before sending. A pre-IRN PDF is not a valid B2B invoice for a recipient claiming input tax credit.
  • Caption need not duplicate the invoice. A friendly caption ("Aapka Sales Invoice INV-2026-0042 attach kiya hai") is courteous, not mandated. The PDF carries the legal weight.

The privacy angle most articles skip

Compliance is one half. Customer data protection is the other. When you ship a WhatsApp invoice through a cloud relay — most "Tally to WhatsApp" SaaS products do this — you are giving a third party your full customer phone book and a copy of every invoice you issue. Under the DPDP Act, that is a transfer to a data fiduciary you may or may not have a written contract with.

Bottom line

Sending a GST invoice on WhatsApp is well within the GST framework. The compliance burden is on the invoice content and on retention, not on the delivery channel. Your job is to make sure the PDF is right, the original is stored, and your customer's phone number doesn't leak to a vendor who didn't sign your DPA.
